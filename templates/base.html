About us

Africa Startup Ecosystem Builders Society (ASEB Society)

The Africa Startup Ecosystem Builders Society (ASEB Society) is a pan-African “network of networks” uniting ecosystem builders, entrepreneurs, policymakers, and organizations dedicated to advancing inclusive and resilient startup ecosystems across the continent./p>

  • Our Mission

    • To advance startup ecosystem building as a strategic approach to economic development, fostering inclusive, sustainable, and thriving startup ecosystems across Africa.

  • Our Vision

    • We envision a future where African economies are more resilient, equitable, and innovative, powered by strong and interconnected startup ecosystems

Goals

Redefining Pathways for Entrepreneurial Ecosystems

ASEB Society outlines seven transformative objectives to elevate ecosystem building, fostering inclusive, interconnected communities where entrepreneurship thrives. Here’s the reimagined framework:

Diverse Leadership Networks

Amplify underrepresented voices to dismantle inequity. Cultivate leadership from diverse backgrounds to drive equitable access and systemic change.

Key Actions:
Fellowships for marginalized builders, inclusive speaker databases, and resource provider diversity initiatives.

Trust-Driven Partnerships

Bridge divides through collective action. Foster unity across sectors by prioritizing shared resources and transparent collaboration.

Key Actions:
Regional collaboration toolkits, ambassador programs, and culture-building workshops.

Unified Lexicon Development

Align language to accelerate impact. Harmonize terminology and outcomes to streamline cross-field collaboration

Key Actions:
Field-wide mission alignment, dynamic terminology dictionaries, and a universal professional pledge.

Cross-Sector Connectivity

Align language to accelerate impact. Harmonize terminology and outcomes to streamline cross-field collaboration

Key Actions:
Field-wide mission alignment, dynamic terminology dictionaries, and a universal professional pledge.

Impact Measurement Standards

Quantify progress to refine practices. Establish metrics that capture ecosystem complexity and drive innovation.

Key Actions:
Peer learning hubs, case study repositories, and practitioner-researcher conferences.

Global Advocacy Initiatives

Mobilize champions for entrepreneurial ecosystems. Educate policymakers and institutions on entrepreneurship’s societal value.

Key Actions:
Policy playbooks, awareness campaigns, and foundation activation programs.

Professional Ecosystem Frameworks

Empower builders with tools for longevity. Create sustainable career pathways through training, funding, and recognition.

Key Actions:
Certification programs, job boards, and peer mentorship networks.
